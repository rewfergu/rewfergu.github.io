Raspberry Pi Bluetooth Audio Receiver

A Raspberry Pi, with a Bluetooth adapter can receive audio from a variety of sources and output the signal through its built-in audio outputs. Any audio device with an aux jack could potentially receive a wireless Bluetooth audio signal.

Instructables Link

This article on instructables may be a good starting point.
http://www.instructables.com/id/Turn-your-Raspberry-Pi-into-a-Portable-Bluetooth-A/

Modifying files

I can actually pair with the Pi, but no audio.

These are the files I messed with for future reference:

  • /etc/bluetooth/audio.conf
  • /etc/pulse/daemon.conf
  • /etc/bluetooth/main.conf
  • /var/lib/bluetooth//config
  • /etc/udev/rules.d/99-input.rules
  • /usr/lib/udev/bluetooth
  • /etc/init.d/bluetooth-agent
  • /etc/inittab
